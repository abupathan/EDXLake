    Privacy Policy

    Last updated: October 18, 2025

    EduData Exchange (“EDX”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) provides a governed data platform for education institutions and agencies. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, share, and protect information in connection with our SaaS platform and related services.

    Education privacy: EDX is designed to support FERPA, COPPA, PPRA, and applicable state laws, and provides tooling to help customers meet GDPR/UK GDPR and CCPA/CPRA obligations. Your institution remains the data controller (or school official, as applicable); EDX operates as a data processor/service provider.

    Scope & Roles

    This policy covers use of EDX products and websites where this policy is linked. For K-12 and Higher-Ed customers, the institution or district is the data controller (or the agency responsible for student records). EDX acts as:

    • Data Processor / Service Provider for customer-provided data; and
    • Controller for our own business operations data (e.g., billing, product telemetry).

    Information We Collect

    1) Customer-Provided Data

    Data ingested by your tenant via connectors/APIs (“Customer Data”), such as records aligned to Ed-Fi, OneRoster, or CEDS. Customer Data may include personal information and, where authorized, student records.

    2) Account & Support

    • Profile and SSO identifiers (e.g., name, email, org ID), roles, and purposes of use.
    • Support tickets, comments, call/chat transcripts.

    3) Platform Telemetry

    • Events (auth, policy evaluation, API usage), job/pipeline metrics, and error logs.
    • Device & browser metadata (user agent, approximate region); no keystroke logging.

    4) Website & Cookies

    Essential cookies for session and security; optional analytics cookies with consent where required.

    How We Use Information

    • Provide, secure, and operate the platform (auth, policy enforcement, auditing, resiliency).
    • Maintain data quality, lineage, and governance features configured by your institution.
    • Support and improve the service (e.g., performance tuning, feature development).
    • Comply with law, enforce agreements, and prevent misuse or security incidents.

    Legal Bases (GDPR/UK GDPR)

    Where we are a processor, the legal basis is determined by the customer controller (e.g., public interest/official authority in education, legal obligation, legitimate interests, or consent as applicable). For EDX business data, we rely on contract performance, legitimate interests (service improvement, security), legal obligation, or consent where required (e.g., optional analytics cookies).

    Education Privacy

    • FERPA: EDX acts as a “school official” with legitimate educational interests under the customer’s control and written agreement.
    • COPPA: For services directed to children under 13, we operate only at the direction of the school/district or parent where required.
    • PPRA: EDX does not create or administer student surveys; any such data is processed per customer instructions.
    • State laws: EDX supports data protection requirements (e.g., SOPPA/NY Ed Law 2-d) via contracts and technical controls.

    How We Share Information

    • Sub-processors who provide infrastructure or support (listed below), bound by DPA terms.
    • Third-party integrations explicitly enabled by customers (e.g., SSO, analytics destinations).
    • Legal/Safety when required to comply with law or respond to lawful requests.
    • We do not sell personal information and do not use Customer Data to build advertising profiles.

    International Transfers

    Where data are transferred internationally, we use lawful transfer mechanisms (e.g., SCCs, UK IDTA/Addendum) and implement technical and organizational measures aligned to risk.

    Data Retention

    Customer Data retention is governed by your tenant’s policies and agreements. EDX retains platform logs and support records for legitimate business needs and legal obligations, then securely deletes or anonymizes them.

    Security

    • Encryption in transit and at rest; key management aligned with industry standards.
    • Least-privilege access, role-/purpose-based policy enforcement, and row/column masking.
    • Secure SDLC, vulnerability management, and third-party testing.
    • Continuous monitoring and incident response with audit logging.

    Your Privacy Choices & Rights

    Depending on your location and role, you may have rights of access, correction, deletion, portability, restriction, or objection. For student records, please contact your institution; EDX processes requests at the controller’s direction. For EDX business data (e.g., your account), contact us directly as described below.

    Children’s Privacy

    EDX does not offer direct-to-student consumer services. Where Customer Data includes information about children, we process it only per school/agency direction, consistent with FERPA/COPPA and contract obligations.

    Changes to This Policy

    We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect operational, legal, or regulatory changes. Material changes will be communicated through the service or by email where appropriate, with an updated “Last updated” date.
