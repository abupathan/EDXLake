    Accessibility at EDX

    Last updated: October 18, 2025

    EduData Exchange (EDX) is committed to building inclusive products aligned with WCAG 2.2 Level AA and Section 508. This page summarizes our standards, testing, assistive technology support, and how to request accommodations or report issues.

    EDX provides a VPAT® on request and continuously improves accessibility through design reviews, automated checks, and user testing.

    Our Commitment

    • Design and code reviews include accessibility criteria (color/contrast, semantics, focus order).
    • Automated linting plus manual screen reader tests before release.
    • Accessible alternatives for time-based media and complex visualizations.
    • Responsive layouts and reflow support at 200–400% zoom.

    Standards & Scope

    • WCAG 2.2 Level AA (success criteria integrated into our SDLC checklists).
    • Section 508 (U.S.), EN 301 549 (where applicable).
    • Scope includes the EDX web app, public docs pages, and support portal.

    Keyboard Navigation

    • Logical focus order; visible focus styles; skip-to-content link.
    • Modal dialogs trap focus and restore on close.
    • Menus and disclosure widgets use ARIA roles and keyboard patterns.

    Color & Contrast

    EDX meets or exceeds 4.5:1 text contrast for body text and 3:1 for large text. Dark mode and high-contrast themes are available where supported by your tenant settings.

    Non-Text Content

    • Meaningful images include alt text; decorative images are hidden from AT.
    • Charts include data tables or text alternatives where feasible.
    • Icons are labeled via aria-label or visually hidden text.

    Forms & Validation

    • Labels bound to inputs; required fields indicated; inline error messages announced via ARIA live regions.
    • Clear instructions and examples; no reliance on color alone.

    Video/Audio

    • Captions for prerecorded content; transcripts where appropriate.
    • Audio descriptions for essential visual information when feasible.

    Feedback & Contact

    If you encounter accessibility barriers or need an alternative format, email accessibility@edx.example. Please include your browser/OS, assistive tech (if any), steps to reproduce, and screenshots or recordings if possible.

    Requesting Accommodations

    Customers can request reasonable accommodations via their Customer Success Manager or by emailing support@edx.example. We coordinate with the institution’s accessibility office.

    Updates & Versioning

    We review this page at least annually and after significant UI changes. A current VPAT® is available on request.
